Tips on Preventing Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis is a condition characterized by weakened bones that are more susceptible to fractures. It is commonly referred to as a "silent disease" because it can progress without any symptoms until a bone breaks.

Background Info.

Bone is living tissue that constantly breaks down and is replaced. Osteoporosis occurs when the creation of new bone doesn't keep up with the loss of old bone. This leads to decreased bone mass and strength. The most common injuries in people with osteoporosis are wrist fractures, hip fractures, and fractures of the spinal vertebrae. This is most commonly found in older generations, where bone structure has slowly deteriorated. More Info Here.

Diet/Exercise

Ensure a diet rich in calcium and vitamin D. Good sources of calcium include dairy products, almonds, broccoli, kale, canned salmon with bones, sardines, and soy products, such as tofu. For vitamin D, sunlight exposure is crucial, and it can also be found in fortified foods and supplements. A good diet will help prevent Osteoporosis from forming.

Regular exercise is critical. Weight-bearing exercises (such as walking, jogging, and climbing stairs) and strength training can help strengthen bones.

Medications

  • If you're at a high risk of fractures, certain medications can help to prevent bone loss and strengthen bones. Consult with a healthcare provider for guidance.
  • Bone Density Tests are also very important for identification

Monitor Hormone Levels

Hormonal imbalances, particularly lower estrogen levels in women post-menopause, significantly affect bone density. Men can also experience bone density loss due to a drop in testosterone. Hormone replacement therapy may be considered under the supervision of a healthcare provider.
