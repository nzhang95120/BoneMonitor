Prevalence

According to the International Osteoporosis Foundation, osteoporosis affects an estimated 75 million people in Europe, USA, and Japan.

Fractures

Osteoporosis causes more than 8.9 million fractures annually worldwide. This means an osteoporotic fracture is occurring every 3 seconds.

Impact on Women

One in three women over age 50 will experience osteoporotic fractures, as will one in five men aged over 50.

Mortality

Approximately 20-25% of people who suffer a hip fracture die within a year following the fracture, and many of those who survive need long-term nursing care.

Info

Background Info

Osteoporosis translates to "porous bone." Viewed under a microscope, healthy bone looks like a honeycomb. When osteoporosis occurs, the holes and spaces in the honeycomb are much larger than in a healthy bone. Osteoporotic bones have lost density or mass and contain abnormal tissue structure. As bones become less dense, they weaken and are more likely to break.

Economic Impact

Global Costs: The worldwide expense related to osteoporosis, especially due to fracture treatment, is estimated to exceed $19 billion annually in the U.S. alone, with projections suggesting it could reach over $25.3 billion by 2025. Impact on Productivity

Loss of Workdays: Fractures can lead to a substantial decrease in productivity, with patients often requiring weeks to months for recovery. The indirect costs through lost earnings and productivity can be substantial. Long-Term Disability: Some patients with hip fractures never regain full mobility, leading to long-term disability and increased dependency, further escalating care costs.

Early Diagnosis

Diagnosing osteoporosis early is crucial for managing the disease effectively and preventing fractures. Early diagnosis allows for interventions that can significantly slow the progression of bone loss and minimize the risk of fractures.

Early detection of osteoporosis is important because it typically doesn't cause noticeable symptoms until a bone breaks. Identifying the disease before a fracture occurs can help. This is why VisiBone is Necessary.

DEXA (dual-energy x-ray absorptiometry) scans are the gold standard for measuring bone mineral density (BMD).

FRAX Tool

The FRAX tool uses individual patient models to estimate the 10-year probability of a hip fracture or other major osteoporotic fracture, aiding in the management and treatment decisions.
